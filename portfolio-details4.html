Emissions and Oil/Gas Wells on Cardiovascular Mortality

Investigated the environmental impact of toxic emissions from facilities and their correlation with cardiovascular disease mortality in Washington state. The project aimed to assess the public health implications of oil and gas well locations.

My Role

  • Used QGIS to analyze and visualize toxic release estimations (RSEI), cardiovascular mortality rates, and oil/gas well locations.
  • Created graduated color ramps to represent RSEI risk scores and dot density maps to visualize cardiovascular mortality rates.
  • Conducted buffer analysis by creating a 10-mile radius around oil and gas wells to assess potential health impacts.
  • Joined multiple datasets, including census tract shapefiles, to provide a comprehensive analysis of environmental and health data.

Results:

  • Identified regions with higher cardiovascular mortality rates within proximity to oil and gas wells, highlighting potential environmental health risks.
  • Delivered actionable insights for public health officials to target interventions and reduce health disparities.

Tools used: QGIS, spatial analysis, buffer analysis, data visualization, geospatial databases
