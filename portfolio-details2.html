HR General Analytics

Analyzed a comprehensive HR dataset sourced from Kaggle to uncover trends in employee attrition, performance metrics, and workforce demographics. The project aimed to provide actionable insights for HR professionals, data scientists, and business analysts, enabling better workforce planning and strategy development.

  • Code: Github
  • Visualization: Tableau Public

My Role

  • Conducted exploratory data analysis (EDA) using Python to identify key trends in employee attrition, job roles, salaries, and commute distances.
  • Designed and developed an interactive Tableau dashboard to visualize workforce metrics, including KPIs and demographic insights.
  • Created interactive visualizations that allow users to filter and explore data dynamically, enhancing usability and insights.
  • Focused on key HR metrics such as average years in the company, average years for promotion, and average years in the current role to provide actionable insights for retention strategies.

Key Insights

  • Discovered that employees in certain job roles (e.g., Sales, Technology) were more likely to leave, with salary disparities and lack of promotions being key contributing factors.
  • Calculated the average commute distance (22.5 km or 13.98 miles) and found a correlation between longer commutes and higher attrition rates.

Tableau Dashboard Highlights

  • KPI
    1. Average Years in Company
    2. Average Years for Promotion
    3. Average Years in Current Role
  • Interactive Visualizations
    1. Pie Chart: Showcased the distribution of employees across fields (HR, Sales, Tech).
    2. US Map: Highlighted the states where HR jobs are concentrated (California, New York, Illinois).
    3. Box Plot/Whisker Plot: Visualized salary ranges for different job positions.
    4. Bar Graph: Displayed the education levels of employees in these roles.
  • Interactivity: Users can click on visualizations to filter data dynamically, enabling deeper exploration of trends and patterns.

Tools Used: Python, Tableau, Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, Seaborn, data visualization
